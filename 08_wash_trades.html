The DSI → AURIGA Pipeline

Die DSI → AURIGA Pipeline

One of the most striking patterns in the share register is the apparent transfer pipeline from DSI Digital Solutions Invest GmbH to AURIGA Capital AG. DSI held 1,699,987 shares at end-2020, then lost exactly 849,994 in December 2021. One year later, AURIGA — controlled by the Steiner family — appeared in the register with exactly 849,994 shares. The Steiner family already controlled 30% of BOSYNC through AURIGA, meaning the DSI shares effectively circled back to entities under the same beneficial ownership orbit.

By end-2023, AURIGA had distributed over 1.1 million shares to entities including ZKP Innovations (512,718), Lapis Ltd. (285,000), Attegia Holding (162,045), and others — all vehicles controlled by UBOs also represented in the broader SyncPilot structure. The pattern is consistent with share parking: moving ownership through intermediaries to obscure concentration of control.

Legal Framework

Rechtlicher Rahmen

Applicable Provisions

Anwendbare Vorschriften

Scheingeschäft — §117 BGB
A declaration of intent made to another party with their agreement that it is merely illusory (zum Schein) is void. Transactions recorded in the name of a non-existent entity constitute Scheingeschäfte by definition — the purported recipient cannot have legal capacity to receive shares.
Eine Willenserklärung, die einem anderen gegenüber mit dessen Einverständnis nur zum Schein abgegeben wird, ist nichtig. Übertragungen an eine nicht existierende Gesellschaft sind per definitionem Scheingeschäfte.
Wash Trading — EU MAR Art. 12(1)(a)
Under the EU Market Abuse Regulation (596/2014), transactions that give misleading signals about supply, demand, or price of financial instruments constitute market manipulation. While SyncPilot is not publicly listed, the circular transfer patterns — entities transferring shares to affiliated vehicles and back — mirror wash-trading structures that would be prohibited in regulated markets.
Strohmanngeschäft — Nominee Arrangements
The AURIGA-BOSYNC interlock, where the Steiner family controls AURIGA (100%) which holds 30% of BOSYNC, while Bleicher holds the remaining 70%, creates a Strohmannkonstellation (nominee arrangement). The beneficial ownership is concentrated but the legal ownership appears distributed.
Verschleierung der wirtschaftlichen Berechtigung — GwG §§18–25
The Geldwäschegesetz (Anti-Money Laundering Act) requires transparent disclosure of ultimate beneficial owners. Routing ownership through four Tiven entities across three jurisdictions, terminating in a non-existent entity, directly contravenes these transparency requirements.
Konzernrecht — AktG §§15–22
German group law provisions establish that enterprises under common control (verbundene Unternehmen) must be identified. The BOSYNC-AURIGA-DSI-Tiven web constitutes a de facto group (faktischer Konzern) without the formal Beherrschungsvertrag (domination agreement) or loss-compensation obligations that AktG §302 would require.
